# Recency Flags — Where the Field May Have Moved Past the Repo

**Started:** July 2, 2026 (Fable), per Commission I. This is a flagging file, not a rewrite
order: each entry = what the series says → what may have changed → confidence → suggested
handling. Nothing here is applied to prose without the writer's sign-off (or an execution-spec
item). Sources: the Day-4 research pass (sonnet agents, confidence flags preserved), the Miano
transcript (writer-supplied, digested in `04-Working-Notes/miano-notes.md`), and Fable's own
training knowledge (cutoff early 2026 — treat as a lead, not a citation).

## F-01 — First-city dating: the prologue's "around 3500 BCE" runs late ★
**Series says:** "the first cities in the valley of the Tigris and Euphrates, somewhere around
3500 BCE" (prologue). **Field:** Tell Brak urban by ~3800; Uruk a full city by ~3700 (Miano,
consistent with mainstream syntheses). **Confidence: high** that 3500 undersells it.
**Handling:** one-word-class fix — "somewhere before 3500 BCE" or "around 3700." Writer decides;
add to a future prologue touch-pass, not the Opus pilot.

## F-02 — Cities-began-in-the-south needs its northern clause ★
**Series says:** P2's why-here architecture is all southern. **Field:** Tell Brak (and Hamoukar's
town phase) put large-scale settlement in the north as early as or earlier than the south — the
"southern cradle" is now a contested simplification (Miano; McMahon; Ur). **Confidence: high.**
**Handling:** the one-sentence honesty clause drafted in the glimpse bank (P2): the south is
where the full package assembles — writing, the temple economy, the city *system* — but the
north may have started the race. Strengthens, not weakens, the poverty-paradox argument (Brak
shows size; the south shows the machine).

## F-03 — Uruk's "temples" are now hedged as assembly halls with cultic functions
**Series says:** P2 renders the Eanna institution as "the temple" throughout (temple-economy
framing). **Field:** the monumental Eanna/Kullab buildings are "more recently understood as
public assembly halls with associated cultic functions" (Miano's phrasing). The temple-*economy*
model itself rests on the archaic texts and survives; the *buildings'* identification is what
moved. **Confidence: medium.** **Handling:** optional half-clause in P2 ("buildings we call
temples — though the field argues about what happened inside them"); the "granary with a god on
top" line survives either way.

## F-04 — Göbekli deliberate-backfill is contested
**Series says:** P1 (and spine 08) lean on deliberate, careful burial. **Field:** post-Schmidt
work attributes some infill to slope-wash/erosion; intentionality is argued case-by-case.
**Confidence: high that it's contested.** **Handling:** P1 already hedges the site's mythology;
the spine's P1 chord (08) carries its own hedge instruction. Keep the hedge; don't drop the beat.

## F-06 — Botai horses are not the ancestors of domestic horses
**Relevant if** any steppe material implies Botai-as-first-domestication. **Field:** 2018 aDNA:
Botai horses = Przewalski lineage; modern domestic line (DOM2) spreads after ~2200 BCE (Librado
2021). **Confidence: high (well-attested).** **Handling:** the glimpse bank's Sintashta entry is
already written to the new consensus; flag exists so no one imports the older story from a
pre-2018 source.

## F-07 — Nebra Sky Disc: a minority redating exists
Majority holds ~1600 BCE; Gebhard & Krause (2020) argued Iron Age, rebutted by most specialists.
**Confidence: majority view stable.** **Handling:** if the disc is used (glimpse bank alternate),
one hedging clause; don't lead with the dispute.

## F-08 — Thera eruption chronology (pre-emptive flag)
The series has so far avoided staging Thera. If it ever does: the radiocarbon-vs-archaeological
dating gap (~1600s vs ~1500s BCE) is one of the field's famous open wounds, with post-2018
tree-ring work narrowing but not closing it. **Confidence: dispute is live.** **Handling:** the
beat-menus file already recommends not staging Thera without a dedicated research pass. Keep it
that way.

## F-09 — The "Gulf Oasis" hypothesis is speculative (imported from Cooper's episode)
Cooper airs the postglacial-flooding origin story for the Sumerians (the Gulf as a drowned river
valley; the flood myth as memory; Dilmun) *explicitly labeled as speculation he finds
attractive*. **Field:** it's a real published hypothesis with no consensus. **Handling:** if the
series ever wants it (P2's flood/Gilgamesh material is the natural site), it must wear the same
label Cooper gives it — a story scholars tell around the evidence's edges, not a finding. Low
priority; the series' flood material already works without it.

## F-10 — 4.2 ka event and Akkad's fall (standing flag)
P3 ties Akkad's collapse to drought-era pressures. The megadrought-caused-the-collapse framing
has both strong proxy evidence and prominent skeptics (correlation/dating-resolution critiques).
**Confidence: dispute is live.** **Handling:** verify P3's current hedge level during the Opus
pilot's P3 pass — the safe shape is "a drying century is in the sediment cores; what it did to
the empire is argued."
