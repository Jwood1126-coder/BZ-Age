Positions taken during the Fable window, with rationale and reversal cost. Successor models:
**do not reopen these silently.** If new evidence or the writer's instinct pushes against one,

Format per entry: the call · why · what reversing it would cost.

## D-001 — Draft versioning is append-only
Every draft version is preserved beside its successor (draft-03 stays when draft-04 lands).
**Why:** the writer's standing instruction; the version trail is the project's memory.
**Reversal cost:** loss of the record; violates a direct instruction. Do not reverse.

## D-002 — One voice holds the pen
No model or agent fans out prose-writing. Ideas, architecture, and example sentences may come from
anywhere; finished narration comes from the single narrator voice.
**Why:** master prompt non-negotiable #2; audible seams are fatal in audio.
**Reversal cost:** a series that sounds stitched. Do not reverse.

## D-004 — Food-origins material is framed through storage, labor, logistics, tax, and climate
Not through plant or crop science.
**Why:** a safety filter once tripped on crop-science framing and derailed a session; the
storage/labor lens is also simply the better *story* lens for this series.
**Reversal cost:** session derailment risk. Do not reverse.

## D-008 — The Xenophon homage: own it, in launch copy, never in-script
The opening's *Kings of Kings* resemblance is acknowledged in one line of show notes; the script
never breaks the storyteller's fiction to cite an influence.
**Why:** the differentiation already exists structurally — P9 out-builds the borrowed image (the
library under his feet; the answers eventually read). Acknowledged homage reads as lineage;
concealment is the only losing move. Full argument: `07-bookends.md` Part II.
**Reversal cost:** low (edit launch copy) — but do not move the acknowledgment into the script.

## D-009 — The mission is stated once, in the prologue, in-world — then earned, never re-stated
"The Greeks themselves stood in the shadow of something older" lives in the prologue's
told-by-people-who-didn't-know line and nowhere else as thesis. The reception spine is the earning
mechanism thereafter. (Provisional pending `08-reception-spine.md`; treat as settled unless the
spine work overturns it.)
**Why:** a repeated mission drumbeat is the "this series" tic at series scale; a buried mission
gives the listener no reason-to-care until hour three. One statement, hour zero.
**Reversal cost:** moderate — re-litigating this reopens the self-reference purge.

## D-010 — Reception-spine dosage: one element per part, chord-sized, two scenes only
One reception beat per part (P5 Sphinx and P6 Khaemwaset are the only full scenes before P8–P9);
never announced on air; no verb of reception repeats (the conjugation table in
`08-reception-spine.md` is the enforcement mechanism). Throughlines #9 and #10 merge into this
device; their files remain as philosophy.
**Why:** the device must stay a renewable source of awe, not become the series' next tic; the
clock-test governs it like every other device.
**Reversal cost:** low per-beat; high if the discipline lapses (retro-fitting dedup across nine
parts).
