# Part 1 — Open Research Questions

Open questions specific to *Before Bronze*. Update as research surfaces answers or as new questions emerge.

---

## How recent is the bioarchaeological consensus on the agricultural revolution's costs?
- **Raised:** 2026-05-26
- **What would close it:** Recent (post-2015) review article on Neolithic transition health markers. Confirm the Cohen-Armelagos framing has held up vs. recent revisions.
- **Why it matters:** The agricultural-revolution-as-bad-deal beat is the opening contrarian-honest move of the series. If the consensus has shifted, the beat needs updating.

## Is the Göbekli Tepe "ritual before agriculture" narrative holding up?
- **Raised:** 2026-05-26
- **What would close it:** Recent excavation reports and synthesis pieces. Klaus Schmidt's framing has been challenged; need to know where the field is now.
- **Why it matters:** Lesser-known-fact #6 in the Part 1 anticipated-questions list depends on this. If the framing has been revised, restate.

## How much of the Varna grave goods narrative is settled?
- **Raised:** 2026-05-26
- **What would close it:** Recent treatments of the Varna necropolis and early Balkan/Carpathian metallurgy.
- **Why it matters:** The Varna gold is one of the best visual hooks for the early-metallurgy beat.

## Current best account of how bronze metallurgy spread?
- **Raised:** 2026-05-26
- **What would close it:** Recent synthesis on bronze metallurgy's diffusion vs. independent invention.
- **Why it matters:** Affects how Part 1 transitions to Part 2 — the question of whether the technological foundation for cities was diffused or convergent.

## Is the arsenical-bronze poisoning narrative overstated?
- **Raised:** 2026-05-26
- **What would close it:** Skeletal evidence from copper-age metalworking sites; review of the arsenicosis claims.
- **Why it matters:** Lesser-known-fact #5 in Part 1 anticipated questions. The hammered point about toxic early metalwork should be calibrated to what the evidence actually supports.
