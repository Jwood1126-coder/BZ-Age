# Editorial Stance — Contrarian-Honest

## What we are committing to

The series tells the Bronze Age the way the evidence actually tells it, not the way pop history has rounded it off, even when that means saying things that defy the expected story.

## What we are not

Not contrarian for its own sake. Not the reactive "actually, everything you know is wrong" pose. That ages badly because it's defined by the consensus it's against — and Bronze Age studies is a field where the consensus shifts constantly. Today's contrarian take is tomorrow's mainstream is the day after tomorrow's outdated mainstream that was already overturned by the time the pop-history book hit shelves.

## The discipline

When the evidence says something uncomfortable, sit with the discomfort. When the evidence contradicts a beloved narrative, contradict the narrative. When the evidence reveals something genuinely surprising, let the surprise land instead of pre-explaining it away. **The shock is a byproduct of honesty, not the goal.**

## The sister discipline — anti-sensationalism

Some material is going to be genuinely shocking. The brutality. The slavery. The inversions of expected narratives. The shock has to be earning analytical work, not just goosing the listener. If a contrarian beat is in the script because it's true and important, it earns its place. If it's in the script because it's startling, it doesn't.

## How this serves storytelling

Honest storytelling is more powerful than rounded-off storytelling. The listener can tell when they're being told a tidy version of a messy story; they trust the messy version more. The contrarian-honest stance is what allows the series to access the surprising, the disturbing, the inverting moves that make great stories great.

But the stance is in service of the story. When a contrarian beat would kill a story rather than make it stronger — and when the honest version is genuinely less interesting than a slightly simplified version — we make judgment calls. The point is not to maximize how much we disagree with pop history. The point is to tell the best story we can while being honest.
