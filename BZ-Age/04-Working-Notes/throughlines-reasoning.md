# Throughlines — Reasoning

Why these, in this form.

## The strongest of the bunch

### Fragility of complexity
The Bronze Age is the first case study in cascading systemic failure in the human record. Lets the whole series talk to the present without strain. Should be the spine.

### The first globalization
Will land hard with listeners because most have no idea this was true. The Uluburun shipwreck alone is a Carlin scene waiting to happen.

### Literacy as a losable technology
Deeper than it first appears. Not just that Greece forgot how to write — the Linear B script was specifically a tool for administering palace economies, and when the palaces died, the script had no purpose. Writing wasn't lost as a general capacity; it was lost as a function.

## Reformulated

### "The invisible people" → "the ground-level world"
The original formulation was methodological and defensive — "remember the people the records don't see." The reformulation makes it generative: the ground-level world is not a supplement to the main story. It **is** the main story.

This reframing is what made Hard Rule 8 possible. If the ground-level material is supplementary, you can't require it. If it is primary, you must.

### "The deep weirdness of early statehood" — kept but refocused
Original framing risked becoming aesthetic — "look how strange they were!" To make it pay off, it has to do specific analytical work — for example, showing how the temple-state structure of Sumer made certain kinds of resilience possible and certain kinds impossible.

## Under reconsideration

### The chariot
Real, but somewhat oversold in pop-history. The more interesting story may be the chariot's fall — when massed infantry tactics rendered the elite chariot armies obsolete. Possibly merge into the fragility-of-complexity throughline.

## Added later

### The metallurgical constraint
The Bronze Age was built on a supply chain it could not control. Deep version of the fragility argument.

### The agricultural baseline / calorie ceiling
Connects to invisible majority, climate, contemporary food security.

### The invention of historical memory itself
History invented and weaponized at the same time. Connects to the long-echo throughline but in a different register — the deliberate construction of the past during the period.

## What may emerge

As research deepens, expect one or two more to surface. Possible candidates:
- Religion as state infrastructure (not separable from politics, economics, or law)
- Diplomacy as a real institution
- The gendered economy (Bronze Age production was heavily female, especially textiles)

Speculative. Note them and let the research decide.
