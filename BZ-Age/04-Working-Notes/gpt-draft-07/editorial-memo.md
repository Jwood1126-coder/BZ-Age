# GPT Draft-07 Experimental Pass — Editorial Memo

Date: July 7, 2026

## 1. What GPT Draft-07 Tried To Improve

GPT Draft-07 tries to make BZ-Age feel less like ten brilliant historical chapters and more like one continuous journey: stored food, city, empire, machinery, interdependence, crisis, collapse, restart, memory, rediscovery.

The core move was radical compression. Instead of preserving the full density of draft-05, the pass asks: what if each part had to carry one clean transformation, one or two unforgettable scenes, and a small number of recurring handles?

The pass especially targeted:

- listener orientation;
- proper-noun burden;
- transitions between systems and bodies;
- the Aegean/Mycenaean throughline;
- the difference between scene color and core spine;
- collapse as a synchronized narrowing rather than a list of bad events;
- the finale as an emotional culmination rather than a museum hallway.

## 2. Biggest Structural Changes

- **Part 2 is sharply shortened** around grain, irrigation, temple economy, writing-as-accounting, Uruk as scale, and the moral cost of ledgers. Wrangel, kur/kalam, and several proof-case detours are not included in GPT-07.
- **Part 4 becomes the assembly floor** for the connected world: tin, caravans, letters, debt, law, chariots, and palace institutions all become machinery for distance.
- **Part 5 de-lists the great-power roster** and uses Uluburun as the governing image. Each power gets a role rather than a mini-encyclopedia entry.
- **Part 6 makes Mitanni the wrong lesson** and frames crisis as the loss of slack under synchronized stress.
- **Part 7 becomes a narrowing corridor**: Ugarit, Emar, Pylos, Hattusa, Egypt, harbors, smiths, scribes, and enslaved/dependent people each show a smaller option set.
- **Part 8 makes aftermath both loss and release**, not just decline.
- **Part 9 makes Indus the humility beat but gives the final seat to the privileged-present vantage.** This is a major GPT choice and should be compared against Fable's preferred ending architecture.

## 3. Where GPT Disagreed With Claude/Fable

- GPT-07 is more willing to cut beloved detail for audio clarity. Fable's draft-06 plan preserves more full-source richness and uses itemized splices; GPT-07 tests a leaner spine.
- GPT-07 skips or radically reduces some Fable-planned P2 banked beats, especially Wrangel and kur/kalam, because Part 2 was the clearest overload risk.
- GPT-07 makes the Prologue more mystery-forward and less explicitly cosmic than Fable draft-06.
- GPT-07 gives Part 9's final word to the privileged modern vantage rather than the Indus locked-room humility. It keeps Indus, but not as the literal final note.
- GPT-07 treats several sections as structural auditions rather than publication prose: useful for merge decisions, but not as final copy.

## 4. Where Claude/Fable Should Probably Be Preserved

Claude/Fable's fuller versions likely preserve valuable texture in:

- P1: Harlan, Göbekli, Çatalhöyük, Old Copper, and metallurgy detail.
- P2: any Fable execution of the long-walk/silt geography and selected banked beats, if it avoids overload.
- P3: Woolley/Royal Cemetery/music-and-games material, which GPT cut for momentum.
- P4: fuller Old Assyrian archive texture and Hammurabi legal texture.
- P5: richer Amarna and Akhenaten nuance, if de-listed.
- P6: warehouse texture and fuller Sea Peoples caution.
- P7: detailed archaeological nuance around destruction claims and the ledger-owned people.
- P8: detailed highland/Philistine/Lefkandi archaeology.
- P9: a fuller Champollion/Smith/Ventris rediscovery sequence and any more powerful Fable coda.

## 5. Biggest Remaining Risks

- **Too compressed:** the pass may read more like an excellent narrative condensation than a true replacement draft.
- **Fact sensitivity:** many `[VERIFY]` tags remain, especially where compression turns careful scholarship into sharper sentences.
- **Voice consistency:** all parts are conversational, but the radical compression makes them less lush than draft-05.
- **Loss of wonder through cutting:** some of the original project's best magic lives in abundant specific detail. GPT-07 sometimes trades that away.
- **Final-seat decision unresolved:** Indus as final humility versus privileged-present as final vantage needs a listener test.

## 6. Recommended Next Action Before Recording

Do not record from GPT-07 as-is. Instead:

1. Compare each GPT-07 part against draft-05 and any completed Fable draft-06.
2. Use GPT-07 as a structural/audio clarity layer: borrow spines, transitions, role handles, compression strategies, and final-movement architecture.
3. Preserve Fable/draft-05 texture where it carries wonder, evidence, or emotional specificity.
4. Run a fact-check pass on every `[VERIFY]` item.
5. Do a read-aloud test of Parts 2, 5, 7, and 9 first; those are the places GPT-07 most strongly changed the listener experience.
