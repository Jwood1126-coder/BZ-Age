# Draft-04 Brief — Writer's Feedback Contract

**Date:** June 12, 2026
**Source:** Jake's notes after sampling the prologue through the Epic of Gilgamesh (draft-03, audio assembly).
**Status:** Governing document for the draft-04 pass. Draft-03 files are preserved untouched alongside; every part revised in this pass gets a `draft-04.md`.
**Verdict carried forward:** "There are parts that are very very good... The details and asides are superb and there are a lot of things being put together here that I really like and feel like most people don't do when they write about this subject." The fixes below are about flow, voice residue, and fabric — not conception.

## A. Voice fixes (mechanical, series-wide)

1. **Kill every "a ten-year-old would ask" callout.** Three instances, all Part 1 (lines ~57, ~153, ~195). That framing was editorial guidance, never script copy. Ask the question plainly; no meta-frame announcing that it's a basic question.
2. **De-AI residue, second sweep.** Jake: "Parts of it still read like AI in the pattern of speech." Hunt the v1.0 catalog again (announced scaffolding, "Here is the thing/what...", told-contrasts "Not X. Not Y. But Z.", paragraph-final aphorism bows, instruction-to-listener phrasing, triadic cadences) — plus the subtler tells: over-regular sentence rhythm, "And that matters," rhetorical-question density.
3. **Strip intention-narration.** ~31 "this series / the series" references in prose. Triage: references that *do narrative work in-world* (the prologue's closing declaration; "I am saving it, deliberately, for the very end") may stay; references that narrate the series' own methods or virtues ("this series doesn't launder uncertainty", "the series has shown you this ledger three times now", "keep that in view whenever this series shows you something magnificent") get cut or recast as direct statements. Target: ≤1 per part outside the prologue, and zero that praise the series' own discipline.

## B. Orientation fixes

4. **Expand the series introduction.** The prologue's close keeps the declaration but grows a little: what this series is, what it aims to do, how it will move (without trailer-voice — it must still be a person talking after the Xenophon mystery has earned the right to speak plainly).
5. **Temporal signposting throughout.** The listener must always feel when they are and how much time is passing. The Neolithic→cities jump specifically needs the centuries made palpable (P1 close / P2 open). Device: recurring time-checks in-voice ("that morning by the storehouse was two thousand years ago now"), not date recitation.
6. **Topic selection made implicit.** No on-air justification of why Uruk and not X. The narrative-of-humanity frame carries the choice invisibly. Audit for any "we focus on / we choose / the reason we're looking at" constructions.

## C. Fabric additions (research-gated; weave, don't lecture)

7. **Parallel-world glimpses, especially Greece.** "What was happening in Greece at this time?" — Greece before the Greeks, planted early (P1/P2 era: farming villages, no palaces, no Greek language yet by most reconstructions), recurring in one-breath glimpses that pay forward to Mycenae (P5) and the Dark Age (P8). Same trick available for one or two other familiar places (Britain/Stonehenge already present).
8. **Daily-life texture, threaded into existing beats (not new lectures):** proportion of population living in cities and how it changed over time; what buildings looked like; how ordinary people got goods (markets? stores? the answer is genuinely interesting — ration/redistribution + street vendors + the scholarly debate about marketplaces); food; music; leisure and games; public life; dress. Targets: P2 (Uruk portrait + snapshot absorb most of this), P4 (Old Babylonian city life), P5 (cosmopolitan texture exists already).
9. **Sacred marriage recalibrated.** Jake: "I may have pushed a little too far... I thought the king was having sex with a wooden god; not as exciting if it is a metaphor or just with a priestess." Keep the sequence (it's protected and good history) but tune the build-up so it doesn't promise more shock than the hedged reality delivers; the genuinely strange part — the city as audience to its own theology, the en-priestess AS the goddess — carries the beat. No sensational wind-up.

## D. Structure

10. **Flow and organization as narrative.** Tighten transitions; every section hands off as story, not topics. The whole-series read already mapped drift points; the draft-04 pass executes against them plus the fresh audit reports.
