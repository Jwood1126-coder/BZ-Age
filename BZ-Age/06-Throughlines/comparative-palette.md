# Comparative Palette

The figures, civilizations, and modern parallels available for comparison across the series. Use selectively — comparison clutter is a real failure mode.

## Civilizational comparisons

### Ancient (outside the focus period)
- Han China — bureaucratic states, persistent written tradition
- Classical Greece — the polis as different political form
- Rome — empire's durability, tribute systems
- The Maya — collapse, monumental kingship
- The Inca — empire without writing in the modern sense
- Vedic India — the eastern edge of the Bronze Age world
- Pre-Columbian North America — complex societies without Old World urbanism

### Medieval and early modern
- Feudal Europe — low-amplitude post-collapse worlds
- Mongol Empire — sudden multi-ethnic empire
- Ottoman Empire — long-lived multi-ethnic empire
- Ming China — centralized states under climate stress
- Aztec / Inca at contact — collapse-from-outside (different structure than Bronze Age)
- Hanseatic League — long-distance trade networks
- Renaissance Italian merchant houses — for Old Assyrian comparison

### Modern
- World Wars — sudden destruction of established orders
- Cold War — multi-polar diplomatic system
- Decolonization — reorganization after imperial fall
- 2008 financial crisis — cascading systemic failure
- COVID supply shocks — global supply chain fragility
- Climate stress on fragile states — climate-collapse model
- Digital revolution — losability of literacy parallel

## Figure comparisons

- **Hammurabi** ↔ Justinian, Napoleon, the framers — codifying law
- **Akhenaten** ↔ Constantine, Buddha, early caliphs, Luther — religious revolutionaries
- **Ramses II** ↔ Augustus, Louis XIV, Stalin — political construction of the ruler
- **Sargon of Akkad** ↔ rise-from-obscurity founder figures
- **The Sea Peoples** ↔ Goths and Rome, Mongols, Bantu migrations, modern climate-driven displacement
- **Enheduanna** ↔ Sappho, the Trotula, Hildegard — early known women's voices

## Use sparingly

- Direct equation of Bronze Age divine kingship with modern theocracy (structures too different)
- Atlantis (too crank-adjacent)
- Direct comparison of Bronze Age collapse to Rome's fall (structurally distinct)

## The discipline

For each comparison: flag whether it's doing **analytical work** (illuminating structure or causation) or **rhetorical work** (making the unfamiliar feel familiar). Both are valid. Treating one as the other is the failure mode.
