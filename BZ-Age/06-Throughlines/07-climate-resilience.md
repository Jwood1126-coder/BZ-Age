# Climate and the Limits of Resilience

**Status:** Active throughline.

## What it is

The most sophisticated societies of their age could not adapt fast enough to climate stress. The drought signal in Eastern Mediterranean proxy records around 1200 BCE. The cooling. The volcanic events.

Recent climate science has substantially rewritten the collapse story.

## Why it matters

The contemporary parallel needs no spelling out. The Bronze Age is the first case study in climate-driven civilizational stress.

But the throughline also does analytical work for the older periods — the 4.2-kiloyear event and the Akkadian collapse, the climate variability in the Middle Bronze Age, the regional shifts in agricultural viability.

## Hazards

- Climate-determinist explanations are seductive and have become fashionable. Resist the single-cause story. Climate was a factor, often a major one, but it interacted with internal political dynamics, supply-chain dependencies, succession crises, and migrations.
- The pop-history version of "climate caused the Bronze Age collapse" is too tidy. The honest version is "climate stress was one of multiple converging factors that the system, already strained, could not absorb."
